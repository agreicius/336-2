Theorem 1.13.12. Unique factorization in \(\Z[i]\).

Let \(\alpha\in \Z[i]\) be a nonzero non-unit element.
  1. Factorization into irreducibles.
    There are irreducible elements \(\pi_1,\pi_2,\dots, \pi_r\) of \(\Z[i]\) satisfying
    \begin{align} \alpha \amp =\pi_1\pi_2\cdots \pi_r\text{.}\tag{1.13.2} \end{align}
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Uniqueness of factorization.
    If we have
    \begin{equation*} \alpha=\pi_1\pi_2\cdots \pi_r=\pi_1'\pi_2'\cdots \pi_s'\text{,} \end{equation*}
    with \(\pi_i\) and \(\pi_j'\) irreducible for all \(i\) and \(j\text{,}\) then \(r=s\text{,}\) and after a reordering, we have \(\pi_i'=u\pi_i\) for some unit \(u\in (\Z[i])^*=\{1,-1,i,-i\}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    In particular, the number of irreducible factors in an irreducible factorization of \(\alpha\) is unique, and the irreducible factors are unique up to multiplication by a unit.
    🔗
    🔗
🔗
🔗
