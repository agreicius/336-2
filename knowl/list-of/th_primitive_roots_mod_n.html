Theorem 1.4.1. Primitive roots modulo \(n\).

Let \(n\) be a positive integer. There is a primitive root for \((\Z/n\Z)^*\) if and only if one of the following conditions holds:
  • \(n=2,4\text{;}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(n=p^k\) for some odd prime \(p\) and positive integer \(k\text{;}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(n=2\, p^k\) for some odd prime \(p\) and positive integer \(k\text{.}\)
