Example 1.13.6. GCD in \(\Z[i]\).

Find a GCD \(\delta\) of \(2\) and \(1+3i\) in \(\Z[i]\text{,}\) and write \(\delta=2\alpha+(1+3i)\beta\) for some \(\alpha, \beta\in \Z[i]\text{.}\)
🔗
Solution.
Noting that \(N(2)=4\) and \(N(1+3i)=10\text{,}\) we start the Euclidean algorithm by dividing \(1+3i\) by \(2\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} 1+3i \amp =(1+i)2+(-1+i)\\ 2 \amp =(-1+i)(-1-i)+\boxed{0}\text{.} \end{align*}
We conclude that \(z=-1+i\) is a GCD of \(2\) and \(1+3i\text{.}\) Furthermore, we have
\begin{align*} (-1+i) \amp =-(1+i)2+(1+3i)\text{.} \end{align*}
