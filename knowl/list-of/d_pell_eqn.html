Definition 1.17.1. Pell’s equations.

Let \(d\) be a positive integer that is not a square. The equation
\begin{equation*} x^2-dy^2=1 \end{equation*}
is called Pell’s equation. A generalized Pell equation is any equation of the form
\begin{equation*} x^2-dy^2=n \end{equation*}
where \(n\) is a nonzero integer. The negative Pell equation is the equation
\begin{equation*} x^2-dy^2=-1\text{.} \end{equation*}
