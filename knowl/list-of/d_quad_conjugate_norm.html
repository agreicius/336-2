Definition 1.15.4. Quadratic norm and conjugate.

Let \(d\in \Z-\{0,1\}\) be squarefree, and let \(K=\Q(\sqrt{d})\text{.}\) For any \(\alpha=q_1+q_2\sqrt{d}\in K\text{,}\) we define its conjugate \(\overline{\alpha}\) and norm \(N_K(\alpha)\) as follows:
\begin{align*} \overline{\alpha} \amp =q_1-q_2\sqrt{d}\amp N_K(\alpha)\amp=\alpha\overline{\alpha}=q_1^2-q_2^2\sqrt{d}\text{.} \end{align*}
We call the resulting function \(N_K\colon K\rightarrow \Q\) the norm funcion of \(K\) over \(\Q\text{.}\)
