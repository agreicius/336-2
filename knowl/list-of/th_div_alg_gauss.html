Theorem 1.13.1. Division algorithm in \(\Z[i]\).

Let \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) be Gaussian integers with \(\beta\ne 0\text{.}\) There are Gaussian integers \(q,r\in \Z[i]\) satisfying the following conditions.
  1. \(\alpha=q\beta+r\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. \(0\leq N(r)< N(\beta)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
Moreover, \(q\) and \(r\) can be produced via the following procedure.
  1. Find \(\gamma\in \Q[i]\) satisfying \(\gamma\beta=\alpha\text{:}\) i.e., set \(\gamma=\beta^{-1}\alpha\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Write \(\gamma=q+(s+ti)\text{,}\) where \(q\in \Z[i]\) and \(\{\abs{s},\abs{t}\}\subseteq [0,1/2]\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  3. We have \(\alpha=q\beta+r\text{,}\) where \(r=\alpha-q\beta\) satisfies \(0\leq N(r)< N(\beta)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
🔗
🔗

Proof.

That the procedure above produces a \(q\) and \(r\) in \(\Z[i]\) satisfying \(\alpha=q\beta+r\) is clear. It remains only to show that \(0\leq N(r)< N(\beta)\text{.}\) We have
\begin{align*} N(r) \amp =N(\alpha-q\beta)\\ \amp = N(\gamma\beta-q\beta)\\ \amp =N(\gamma-q)N(\beta)\\ \amp =N(s+ti)N(\beta)\\ \amp =(s^2+t^2)N(\beta)\\ \amp < N(\beta) \amp (s^2+t^2\leq \tfrac{1}{4}+\tfrac{1}{4}=\tfrac{1}{2}) \end{align*}
