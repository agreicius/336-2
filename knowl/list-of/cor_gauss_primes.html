Corollary 1.14.4. Gaussian primes.

Let \(\pi\) be a Gaussian prime.
  1. If \(\pi\mid 2\text{,}\) then \(\pi\) is associate to \(1+i\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Let \(p\) be a prime integer satisfying \(p\equiv 1\pmod 4\text{,}\) and let \(a,b\in \Z\) satisfy \(p=a^2+b^2\text{.}\) If \(\pi\mid p\text{,}\) then \(\pi\) is associate either to the prime \(a+bi\) or the prime \(\overline{a+bi}\text{,}\) but not both.
    🔗
    🔗
  3. Let \(p\) be a prime integer satisfying \(p\equiv 3\pmod 4\text{.}\) If \(\pi\mid p\text{,}\) then \(\pi\) is associate to \(p\text{.}\)

    🔗
