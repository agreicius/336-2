Definition 1.15.1. Quadratic extension of \(\Q\).

Let \(d\in \Z-\{0,1\}\) be a squarefree integer. We define \(\Q(\sqrt{d})\) to be the following subset of \(\C\text{:}\)
\begin{align} \Q(\sqrt{d}) \amp = \{z\in \C\mid z=q_1+q_2\sqrt{d} \text{ for some } q_1,q_2\in \Q\}\text{.}\tag{1.15.1} \end{align}
We call \(\Q(\sqrt{d})\) a quadratic field extension of \(\Q\text{,}\) or a quadratic subfield of \(\C\text{.}\)
🔗
The quadratic field \(\Q(\sqrt{d})\) is real if \(\Q(\sqrt{d})\subseteq \R\) (i.e., if \(d\geq 0\)) and imaginary otherwise (i.e., if \(d< 0\)).
