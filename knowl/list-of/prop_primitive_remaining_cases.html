Proposition 1.4.5. Remaining cases for primitive roots.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer.
  1. If \(n=2p^k\) with \(p\) an odd prime, then \((\Z/n\Z)^*\) has a primitive root.
    🔗
    🔗
  2. If \(n\) is not of the form \(2^k\text{,}\) \(p^k\text{,}\) or \(2p^k\text{,}\) where \(p\) is an odd prime, then \((\Z/n\Z)^*\) does not have a primitive root.
    🔗
    🔗
🔗
