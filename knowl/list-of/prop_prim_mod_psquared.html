Proposition 1.4.2. Primitive roots modulo \(p^2\text{,}\) \(p\) odd.

Let \(p\) be an ood prime. If \([a]_p\) is a primitive root of \((\Z/p\Z)^*\text{,}\) then either \([a]_{p^2}\) or \([a+p]_{p^2}\) is a primitive root of \((\Z/p^2\Z)^*\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗

Proof.

Recall that \(\phi(p^2)=p^2-p=p(p-1)\text{.}\) In your homework, you proved that the function
\begin{align} f\colon \Z/p^2\Z \amp\to \Z/p\Z \tag{1.4.1}\\ [a]_{p^2} \amp \mapsto [a]_{p}\tag{1.4.2} \end{align}
is well-defined and satisfies nice
\begin{align*} f([a]_{p^2}+[b]_{p^2}) \amp = f([a]_{p^2})+f([b]_{p^2}) \amp f([a]_{p^2}\cdot [b]_{p^2})\amp = f([a]_{p_2})\cdot f([b]_{p^2} \end{align*}
for all \(a,b\in \Z\text{.}\) Assume \([a]_p\) is a primitive root modulo \(p\text{,}\) and let \(n=\ord [a]_{p^2}\text{.}\) Since \([a]_{p^2}^n=1=[1]_{p^2}\text{,}\) it follows from the properties above that \([a]_p^n=[1]_p\text{,}\) and thus thus that \(p-1\mid n\) (Proposition 1.2.3). Since furthermore \([a]_{p^2}^{\phi(p^2)}=1\text{,}\) we have \(n\mid p(p-1)\) (Proposition 1.2.3). Since \(\gcd(p,p-1)=1\text{,}\) it follows that \(n=p-1\) or \(n=p(p-1)=\phi(p^2)\text{.}\) In the latter case, \([a]_{p_2}\) is a primitive root modulo \(p^2\text{,}\) and we are done.
🔗
Assume now that \(m=p-1\text{.}\) We show in this case that \([a+p]_{p^2}\) has order \(p(p-1)\text{,}\) and hence is a primitvie root modulo \(p^2\text{.}\) Let \(m'=\ord[a+p]_{p^2}\text{.}\) Since \(a+p\equiv a\pmod p\text{,}\) we know that \([a+p]_{p}^{p-1}=[a]_p^{p-1}=[1]_p\text{,}\) and hence, using our same reasoning as above, that \(m'\in \{p-1, p(p-1)\}\text{.}\) It thus suffices to show that \([a+p]_{p^2}^{p-1}\ne [1]_{p^2}\text{.}\) For this we use the binomial theorem:
\begin{align*} (a+p)^{p-1} \amp =a^{p-1}+\sum_{k=1}^{p-1}{p-1\choose k}a^{p-1-k}p^k\\ \amp \equiv a^{p-1}+(p-1)a^{p-2}p \pmod p^2\\ \amp \equiv 1+(p-1)a^{p-2}p \pmod p^2\\ \amp \not\equiv 1\text{,} \end{align*}
since \(p^2\nmid (p-1)a^{p-2}p\text{.}\) It follows that \([a+p]_{p^2}^{p-1}\ne [1]_{p^2}\text{,}\) as desired.
