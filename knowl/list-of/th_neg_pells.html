Theorem 1.17.4 (Koymans, Pagano, 2022). Negative Pell’s equation.

Let \(D\) be the set of all squarefree positive integers \(d> 1\) whose prime divisors are congruent to \(1\) or \(2\) modulo \(4\text{,}\) and let \(D^{-}\) be the set of squarefree positive integers \(d> 1\) for which the negative Pell’s equation \(x^2-dy^2=-1\) has a solution. We have
\begin{align} \lim\limits_{N\to \infty} \frac{\abs{\{d\in D^{-}\mid d\leq N\}}}{\abs{\{d\in D\mid d\leq N\}}}\amp = 1-\prod_{j \text{ odd}}(1-1/2^j) \tag{1.17.1}\\ \amp \approx 58\%\text{.}\tag{1.17.2} \end{align}
