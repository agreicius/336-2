Theorem 1.10.26. Polar form properties.

Let \(z=r(\cos\theta+i\sin\theta)\) and \(w=s(\cos\psi+i\sin\psi)\) where \(r,s\geq 0\text{.}\)
  1. \begin{equation} \abs{z}=r\tag{1.10.7} \end{equation}
    🔗
    🔗
  2. \begin{equation} \overline{z}=r(\cos(-\theta)+i\sin(-\theta))\text{.}\tag{1.10.8} \end{equation}
    🔗
    🔗
  3. \begin{equation} zw=rs(\cos(\theta+\psi)+i\sin(\theta+\psi))\text{.}\tag{1.10.9} \end{equation}
    🔗
    🔗
  4. If \(z\ne 0\text{,}\) then
    \begin{equation} z^{-1}=\frac{1}{r}\left(\cos(-\theta)+i\sin(-\theta)\right)\text{.}\tag{1.10.10} \end{equation}
