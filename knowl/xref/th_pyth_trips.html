Theorem 1.9.4. Primitive Pythagorean triples.

Let \((a,b,c)\) be a primitive Pythagorean triple with \(a\) even. There is a unique pair of positive integers \((m,n)\) satisfying \(m> n\text{,}\) \(\gcd(m,n)=1\text{,}\) \(m\not\equiv n\pmod 2\) such that
\begin{align} a \amp = 2mn \amp b\amp =m^2-n^2 \amp c=m^2+n^2\tag{1.9.1} \end{align}
