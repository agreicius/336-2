Theorem 1.15.17. Norm-Euclidean rings of integers.

Let \(d\in \Z-\{0,1\}\) be squarefree, and let \(K=\Q(\sqrt{d})\text{.}\) If \(\mathcal{O}_K\) is norm-Euclidean then it enjoys the following properties.
  1. Let \(\alpha,\beta\in \mathcal{O}_K\) and assume \(\alpha\ne 0\) or \(\beta\ne 0\text{.}\) There is a GCD \(\delta\) of \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\text{,}\) and we have \(\delta=\gamma \alpha+\epsilon \beta\) for some \(\gamma, \epsilon\in \mathcal{O}_K\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. An element \(\pi\in \mathcal{O}_K\) is prime if and only if it is irreducible.
    🔗
    🔗
  3. If \(\alpha\in \mathcal{O}_K\) is a nonzero non-unit, then it has a factorization \(\alpha=\prod_{i=1}^r\pi_i\) where \(\pi_i\) is irreducible for all \(1\leq i\leq r\text{,}\) and this factorization is unique in the sense of Theorem 1.13.15.
    🔗
    🔗
🔗
🔗

Proof.

The proofs of these statements can be adapted immediately from the proofs of the corresponding results for \(\Z[i]\text{,}\) all of which build on the fact that the norm satisfies the division algorithm.
